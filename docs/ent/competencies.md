# 👂 ENT Competencies

---

- [ ] Approach to acute hearing loss — conductive vs sensorineural
- [ ] Otitis media — acute, chronic, complications

- [ ] Peritonsillar abscess (quinsy) — diagnosis and management
- [ ] Foreign body — ear, nose, throat — approach
- [ ] Hoarseness — differential diagnosis and red flags

- [ ] Anterior rhinoscopy

## Intern

### Knowledge
- [ ] All medical student competencies above
- [ ] Ludwig's angina — diagnosis and emergency management
- [ ] Epiglottitis — adult and paediatric — diagnosis and airway management
- [ ] Deep neck space infections — anatomy, danger spaces, management
- [ ] Sudden sensorineural hearing loss — emergency treatment
- [ ] Nasal fracture — assessment and management

### Clinical Skills
- [ ] Anterior nasal packing for epistaxis
- [ ] Aural toilet and wicking
- [ ] Removal of simple ENT foreign bodies (supervised)

---

!!! danger "Airway compromise"
    Ludwig's angina, epiglottitis, and deep neck infections can rapidly obstruct the airway. Call senior ENT and anaesthetics early — do not wait for complete obstruction.
