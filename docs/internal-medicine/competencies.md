# 🫀 Internal Medicine Competencies

Core knowledge and skills expected at each level of training. Use this as a self-assessment checklist and study guide.

---

## Medical Student (Final Year)

### Knowledge
- [ ] Approach to the breathless patient
- [ ] Approach to chest pain
- [ ] Approach to the jaundiced patient
- [ ] Approach to acute kidney injury
- [ ] Approach to hyponatraemia and hypernatraemia
- [ ] Diagnosis and initial management of DKA
- [ ] Diagnosis and initial management of hypoglycaemia
- [ ] ECG interpretation — rate, rhythm, axis, STEMI, LBBB
- [ ] ABG interpretation — acidosis, alkalosis, compensation
- [ ] CXR interpretation — consolidation, effusion, pneumothorax, cardiomegaly

### Clinical Skills
- [ ] Cardiovascular examination
- [ ] Respiratory examination
- [ ] Abdominal examination
- [ ] Peripheral vascular examination
- [ ] Venepuncture and IV cannulation
- [ ] 12-lead ECG recording

---

## Intern

### Knowledge
- [ ] All medical student competencies above
- [ ] Management of acute pulmonary oedema
- [ ] Management of hypertensive emergencies
- [ ] Management of upper GI bleed
- [ ] Management of sepsis (Surviving Sepsis Campaign)
- [ ] Antibiotic stewardship principles
- [ ] Interpretation of renal function and electrolytes
- [ ] Warfarin management and INR interpretation
- [ ] DVT/PE diagnosis and anticoagulation
- [ ] Approach to anaemia

### Clinical Skills
- [ ] ABG sampling (radial artery)
- [ ] Nasogastric tube insertion
- [ ] Urinary catheterisation
- [ ] Ascitic tap (diagnostic)
- [ ] Pleural tap (diagnostic)
- [ ] Blood cultures (correct technique)
- [ ] Prescription writing — fluids, analgesia, antibiotics

### Wits-Specific Protocols
- [ ] Wits DKA protocol
- [ ] Wits fluid resuscitation guidelines
- [ ] Charlotte Maxeke/CHBAH antibiotic guidelines

---

## Junior Doctor (Post-internship / Community Service)

### Knowledge
- [ ] All intern competencies above
- [ ] Management of acute coronary syndromes
- [ ] Heart failure management — acute and chronic
- [ ] COPD exacerbation management
- [ ] Pneumonia severity assessment (CURB-65)
- [ ] Approach to the immunocompromised patient
- [ ] HIV-related opportunistic infections
- [ ] Tuberculosis diagnosis and treatment
- [ ] Approach to the patient with altered consciousness
- [ ] Renal replacement therapy indications

### Clinical Skills
- [ ] Central venous line insertion (supervised)
- [ ] Lumbar puncture
- [ ] Pleural drain insertion (supervised)
- [ ] Interpreting echocardiogram reports
- [ ] Interpreting CT chest/abdomen reports
