!!! wits-protocol "Wits-Specific Protocol"
    This protocol reflects current practice at Wits-affiliated hospitals (Charlotte Maxeke, Helen Joseph, Chris Hani Baragwanath). Confirm with your department before use.

---

## Diagnostic Criteria

All three must be present:

- Blood glucose **>11 mmol/L** (or known diabetes mellitus)
- Ketonaemia **≥3 mmol/L** or urine ketones **≥2+**
- pH **<7.3** or bicarbonate **<15 mmol/L**

!!! warning "Euglycaemic DKA"
    DKA can occur with near-normal glucose in patients on SGLT-2 inhibitors (e.g. empagliflozin, dapagliflozin). Maintain high clinical suspicion.

---

## Severity Classification

| Severity | pH | Bicarbonate (mmol/L) | GCS |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Mild** | 7.25–7.30 | 15–18 | 15 |
| **Moderate** | 7.00–7.24 | 10–14 | <15 |
| **Severe** | <7.00 | <10 | <12 |

---

## Monitoring

=== "First 4 hours"
    - Hourly capillary blood glucose
    - Hourly urine output (catheterise if anuric or obtunded)
    - Hourly capillary ketones (if available)
    - 2-hourly electrolytes (especially K⁺)
    - Continuous ECG if K⁺ abnormal

=== "4–24 hours"
    - 2-hourly blood glucose
    - 4-hourly electrolytes
    - 4-hourly ABG/VBG

=== "Resolution criteria"
    All three must be met before stopping fixed-rate insulin:
    
    - Blood glucose <14 mmol/L
    - Ketones <0.3 mmol/L or bicarbonate >18 mmol/L
    - pH >7.3

---

## Fluid Resuscitation

Use **0.9% sodium chloride** (normal saline) as follows:

| Time | Volume | Rate |
|---|---|---|
| 0–1h | 1 L | Stat |
| 1–3h | 1 L | Over 2h |
| 3–5h | 1 L | Over 2h |
| 5–9h | 1 L | Over 4h |
| 9–13h | 1 L | Over 4h |

**Switch to 0.45% NaCl + 5% dextrose** when blood glucose falls to <14 mmol/L.

!!! info "Fluid caution"
    Reduce rate in elderly patients, those with CCF or renal impairment. Target urine output 0.5 mL/kg/hr.

---

## Fixed-Rate Insulin Infusion

- **0.1 units/kg/hr** of short-acting insulin (Actrapid/regular insulin) in normal saline
- **Do NOT give a bolus dose**
- Do NOT start insulin until K⁺ ≥3.5 mmol/L

!!! danger "Hypokalaemia risk"
    Insulin drives K⁺ into cells. Starting insulin with K⁺ <3.5 mmol/L can cause life-threatening hypokalaemia and fatal arrhythmia.

---

## Potassium Replacement

| Serum K⁺ (mmol/L) | KCl to add per litre of fluid |
|---|---|
| >5.5 | None |
| 3.5–5.5 | 40 mmol |
| <3.5 | **Stop insulin** — replace K⁺ urgently, recheck before restarting |

---

## Transition to Subcutaneous Insulin

When resolution criteria are met **and** the patient can eat and drink:

1. Give SC short-acting insulin 30 min **before** stopping infusion
2. If on insulin pre-admission, restart usual regimen
3. If new diagnosis, refer to diabetes team for initiation

---

## Precipitating Causes (FIND IT)

| Letter | Cause |
|---|---|
| **F** | Forgot insulin / non-compliance |
| **I** | Infection (most common — UTI, pneumonia) |
| **N** | New diagnosis of DM |
| **D** | Drugs (steroids, SGLT-2 inhibitors, cocaine) |
| **I** | Ischaemia (MI, stroke) |
| **T** | Trauma / surgery |
