# 🤰 Obstetrics & Gynaecology Competencies

---

## Medical Student (Final Year)

### Knowledge
- [ ] Normal pregnancy — physiological changes by trimester
- [ ] Antenatal care schedule and content
- [ ] Approach to antepartum haemorrhage — placenta praevia vs abruption
- [ ] Pre-eclampsia and eclampsia — diagnosis and management
- [ ] Normal labour — stages, monitoring, partogram interpretation
- [ ] Approach to postpartum haemorrhage
- [ ] Common gynaecological conditions — fibroids, ovarian cysts, PID
- [ ] Approach to abnormal uterine bleeding
- [ ] Cervical cancer — screening, HPV, colposcopy
- [ ] Ectopic pregnancy — diagnosis and management

### Clinical Skills
- [ ] Obstetric abdominal examination — symphysis-fundal height, lie, presentation, engagement
- [ ] Fetal heart auscultation
- [ ] Partogram recording and interpretation
- [ ] Speculum examination (observed)

---

## Intern

### Knowledge
- [ ] All medical student competencies above
- [ ] Induction of labour — indications and methods
- [ ] Caesarean section — indications, consent, postoperative care
- [ ] Shoulder dystocia — recognition and manoeuvres (HELPERR)
- [ ] Neonatal resuscitation at delivery
- [ ] Gestational diabetes — screening and management
- [ ] PMTCT — prevention of mother-to-child HIV transmission
- [ ] Contraception — methods, indications, contraindications
- [ ] Termination of pregnancy — legal framework in South Africa
- [ ] Menopausal management basics

### Clinical Skills
- [ ] Vaginal examination in labour
- [ ] Partogram interpretation and action
- [ ] Episiotomy repair (supervised)
- [ ] Instrumental delivery — assisted by senior
- [ ] IUD and implant insertion (supervised)

### Wits-Specific Protocols
- [ ] Wits PPH protocol
- [ ] Wits pre-eclampsia management guideline
- [ ] SA PMTCT guidelines

!!! warning "Obstetric emergencies"
    Shoulder dystocia, PPH, eclampsia, and cord prolapse are time-critical. Know the protocols before you need them.
