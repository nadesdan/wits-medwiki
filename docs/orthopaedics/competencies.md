# 🦴 Orthopaedics Competencies

---

## Medical Student (Final Year)

### Knowledge
- [ ] Fracture description — site, pattern, displacement, angulation, rotation
- [ ] Principles of fracture management — reduce, hold, rehabilitate
- [ ] Common fractures — Colles, NOF, clavicle, ankle
- [ ] Compartment syndrome — diagnosis and management
- [ ] Septic arthritis vs osteomyelitis — diagnosis and emergency management
- [ ] Back pain — red flags requiring urgent investigation
- [ ] Approach to the acutely injured knee
- [ ] Common paediatric fractures — supracondylar, greenstick, physeal injuries

### Clinical Skills
- [ ] Musculoskeletal examination — upper and lower limb
- [ ] Neurovascular assessment of injured limb
- [ ] X-ray interpretation — fracture identification, alignment

---

## Intern

### Knowledge
- [ ] All medical student competencies above
- [ ] Open fractures — classification, emergency management, antibiotic prophylaxis
- [ ] Pelvic fractures — haemodynamic instability, external fixation
- [ ] Spinal injury — ASIA classification, immobilisation
- [ ] Dislocations — shoulder, hip, knee — reduction techniques
- [ ] Plaster of Paris — application principles, complications
- [ ] Orthopaedic implants — screws, plates, nails, arthroplasty — basic principles
- [ ] DVT prophylaxis in orthopaedic patients

### Clinical Skills
- [ ] Backslab application — above and below elbow, above and below knee
- [ ] Wound management in open fractures
- [ ] Skin traction application
- [ ] Joint aspiration — knee (supervised)

---

!!! danger "Compartment syndrome"
    A missed compartment syndrome causes permanent disability. Pain out of proportion, pain on passive stretch, and tense compartments require urgent surgical consultation — do not delay for investigation.
