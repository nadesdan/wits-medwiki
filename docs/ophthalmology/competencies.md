# 👁️ Ophthalmology Competencies

---

## Medical Student (Final Year)

### Knowledge
- [ ] Approach to the red eye — differential diagnosis
- [ ] Approach to acute visual loss — painless vs painful
- [ ] Conjunctivitis — bacterial, viral, allergic — differentiation and treatment
- [ ] Corneal abrasion — diagnosis and management
- [ ] Acute angle-closure glaucoma — recognition and emergency management
- [ ] Anterior uveitis (iritis) — diagnosis and management
- [ ] Retinal detachment — symptoms, risk factors, emergency referral
- [ ] Central retinal artery occlusion — recognition, emergency management
- [ ] Diabetic retinopathy — screening and classification
- [ ] Hypertensive retinopathy — classification

### Clinical Skills
- [ ] Visual acuity testing (Snellen chart)
- [ ] Visual field assessment by confrontation
- [ ] Pupillary assessment — RAPD (relative afferent pupillary defect)
- [ ] Fundoscopy (direct ophthalmoscopy)
- [ ] Eversion of upper eyelid for foreign body

---

## Intern

### Knowledge
- [ ] All medical student competencies above
- [ ] Chemical eye injury — emergency irrigation, pH testing
- [ ] Penetrating eye injury — assessment, do's and don'ts
- [ ] Orbital cellulitis vs preseptal cellulitis — differentiation and management
- [ ] Optic neuritis — association with MS, management

### Clinical Skills
- [ ] Eye irrigation for chemical injury
- [ ] Fluorescein staining and cobalt blue light examination (supervised)
- [ ] Application of eye drops and ointments

---

!!! danger "Ocular emergencies requiring same-day ophthalmology review"
    - Chemical injury (irrigate first, refer immediately)
    - Central retinal artery occlusion (within 4 hours)
    - Acute angle-closure glaucoma
    - Penetrating eye injury
    - Sudden painless visual loss
