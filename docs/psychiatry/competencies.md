# 🧠 Psychiatry Competencies

---

## Medical Student (Final Year)

### Knowledge
- [ ] Mental state examination (MSE) — all components
- [ ] Psychiatric history taking
- [ ] DSM-5/ICD-11 diagnostic categories — overview
- [ ] Schizophrenia — diagnosis, positive and negative symptoms, management
- [ ] Mood disorders — MDD, bipolar I and II, cyclothymia
- [ ] Anxiety disorders — GAD, panic disorder, PTSD, OCD
- [ ] Substance use disorders — alcohol, cannabis, methamphetamine (common in SA)
- [ ] Mental Health Care Act (South Africa) — voluntary vs involuntary admission
- [ ] Suicide risk assessment — risk factors, protective factors, MSE findings

### Clinical Skills
- [ ] Conduct a full psychiatric history
- [ ] Conduct a Mental State Examination
- [ ] Formulate a psychiatric diagnosis using biopsychosocial model
- [ ] Assess suicide risk

---

## Intern

### Knowledge
- [ ] All medical student competencies above
- [ ] Antipsychotics — typical vs atypical, side effects, NMS
- [ ] Antidepressants — SSRIs, SNRIs, TCAs, MAOIs — indications and side effects
- [ ] Mood stabilisers — lithium, valproate, carbamazepine — monitoring
- [ ] Acute behavioural disturbance — rapid tranquilisation protocol
- [ ] Delirium — diagnosis, causes, management
- [ ] Dementia — types, assessment, management
- [ ] Electroconvulsive therapy (ECT) — indications, consent, process
- [ ] Eating disorders — anorexia nervosa, bulimia
- [ ] Child and adolescent psychiatry basics
- [ ] Liaison psychiatry — medically unexplained symptoms

### Clinical Skills
- [ ] Rapid tranquilisation — drug selection and monitoring
- [ ] Mental Health Care Act — completing section 9 (72-hour assessment)
- [ ] De-escalation techniques

### Wits-Specific Protocols
- [ ] Wits acute behavioural disturbance protocol
- [ ] SA Mental Health Care Act process at Wits hospitals

---

!!! info "Mental Health Care Act"
    The South African Mental Health Care Act 17 of 2002 governs involuntary psychiatric treatment. Every intern must be familiar with the process for 72-hour assessment and longer-term involuntary care.
