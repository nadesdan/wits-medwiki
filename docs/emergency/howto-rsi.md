## Indications

- Failure to oxygenate or ventilate
- Airway protection — GCS ≤8, aspiration risk
- Anticipated deterioration (burns, angioedema, major trauma)
- Requirement for controlled ventilation (raised ICP, status epilepticus)

## Contraindications

!!! danger "No absolute contraindications in a dying patient"
    In a patient in extremis, the benefits of securing the airway outweigh all risks.

Relative contraindications requiring modified approach:

- Predicted difficult airway — consider awake intubation
- Suxamethonium contraindications (see below) — use rocuronium

## Equipment (STOP MAID)

| Letter | Equipment |
|---|---|
| **S** | Suction — Yankauer, switched on |
| **T** | Tube — correct size ETT (men: 8.0, women: 7.5), one size smaller |
| **O** | Oxygen — BVM + reservoir, 15L/min |
| **P** | Positioning — ramped, sniffing position |
| **M** | Monitoring — SpO₂, ETCO₂, ECG, BP |
| **A** | Airway adjuncts — laryngoscope (direct + video), bougie, LMA |
| **I** | IV access — large bore, confirmed patent |
| **D** | Drugs — drawn up, labelled (see below) |

## The 7 Ps

### 1. Preparation
Assign roles before starting: **intubator**, **drug nurse**, **cricoid**, **BVM**.
Team brief: *"Plan A is direct laryngoscopy. If I can't intubate, Plan B is video laryngoscopy. If I can't ventilate, we go to surgical airway."*

### 2. Preoxygenation
- 100% O₂ via non-rebreather mask, minimum **3 minutes**
- Target SpO₂ **>94%** before induction
- Sit patient upright (45°) if possible — increases FRC

### 3. Pretreatment *(optional, case-dependent)*
- **Fentanyl 3 µg/kg IV** — attenuates sympathetic response to laryngoscopy; use in suspected raised ICP
- **Atropine 0.02 mg/kg IV** — in children <1 year to prevent bradycardia from suxamethonium

### 4. Paralysis + Induction (simultaneous)
Give induction agent **immediately followed** by neuromuscular blocker.

**Wits ED Standard Doses:**

| Drug | Dose | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Ketamine** (induction) | 1.5–2 mg/kg IV | Preferred; maintains BP; bronchodilator |
| **Propofol** (induction) | 1.5–2 mg/kg IV | Avoid if hypotensive |
| **Suxamethonium** (paralytic) | 1.5 mg/kg IV | Onset 45–60s; duration 8–10 min |
| **Rocuronium** (if Sx CI) | 1.2 mg/kg IV | Onset 60–90s; reversed by sugammadex |

**Suxamethonium contraindications:**
Hyperkalaemia, crush injury >48h, burns >48h, denervation injury, known myopathy, malignant hyperthermia history, pseudocholinesterase deficiency.

### 5. Protection (Cricoid Pressure)
Apply Sellick's manoeuvre once patient loses consciousness.
Release immediately if it impedes laryngoscopy.

### 6. Placement
- Laryngoscopy after fasciculations cease (~45–60s after suxamethonium)
- Pass ETT under direct vision
- Inflate cuff to 20–25 cmH₂O
- Confirm placement: **5-point auscultation** + **waveform capnography** (gold standard)
- Secure tube, note depth at teeth

### 7. Post-Intubation Care

| Action | Detail |
|---|---|
| Ventilator settings | TV 6–8 mL/kg IBW; RR 12–16; FiO₂ 1.0 initially |
| Post-intubation sedation | Morphine 0.1 mg/kg + midazolam 0.05 mg/kg |
| CXR | Confirm tube position (5cm above carina) |
| NG tube | Decompress stomach |
| Head of bed | 30° elevation |

## Failed Airway Algorithm

```
Can't intubate → attempt ×2 maximum
        ↓
Can ventilate with BVM?
   YES → video laryngoscopy or supraglottic airway (LMA)
   NO  → CICO (Can't Intubate Can't Oxygenate)
              ↓
        Surgical airway (scalpel-finger-bougie cricothyroidotomy)
```

!!! danger "CICO is a surgical emergency"
    If SpO₂ is falling and you cannot ventilate, do not attempt intubation a third time. Call for surgical airway immediately.
