# 🚨 Emergency Medicine & Trauma Competencies

---

## Medical Student (Final Year)

### Knowledge
- [ ] ABCDE approach to the critically ill patient
- [ ] Recognition of shock — hypovolaemic, septic, cardiogenic, obstructive
- [ ] Basic trauma assessment — primary and secondary survey
- [ ] Recognition of life-threatening chest injuries — tension pneumothorax, haemothorax, flail chest, open chest wound, cardiac tamponade
- [ ] Approach to the unconscious patient
- [ ] Approach to acute poisoning
- [ ] Triage principles — START triage

### Clinical Skills
- [ ] Basic life support (BLS) — adult and paediatric
- [ ] Bag-mask ventilation
- [ ] Recovery position
- [ ] Haemorrhage control — direct pressure, tourniquet
- [ ] Cervical spine immobilisation

---

## Intern

### Knowledge
- [ ] All medical student competencies above
- [ ] Advanced Trauma Life Support (ATLS) principles
- [ ] Fluid resuscitation in trauma — damage control resuscitation
- [ ] Management of head injury — GCS, ICP, herniation
- [ ] Spinal cord injury — neurogenic vs spinal shock
- [ ] Burns management — TBSA, Parkland formula, inhalation injury
- [ ] Toxicology — paracetamol, organophosphate, TCA, iron overdose
- [ ] Anaphylaxis — recognition and management
- [ ] Rapid sequence intubation (RSI) — indications and drugs
- [ ] Procedural sedation — agents, monitoring, recovery
- [ ] Point-of-care ultrasound (POCUS) — FAST exam principles

### Clinical Skills
- [ ] Advanced life support (ALS) — adult
- [ ] RSI — observe and assist
- [ ] Chest drain insertion (supervised)
- [ ] Needle decompression of tension pneumothorax
- [ ] Pericardiocentesis (supervised)
- [ ] FAST ultrasound (supervised)
- [ ] Fracture splinting and immobilisation
- [ ] Wound irrigation and closure

### Wits-Specific Protocols
- [ ] Wits ED RSI protocol
- [ ] Wits trauma resuscitation protocol
- [ ] CHBAH/Charlotte Maxeke major trauma pathway

---

## Junior Doctor

### Knowledge
- [ ] All intern competencies above
- [ ] Damage control surgery principles
- [ ] Mass casualty incident management
- [ ] Paediatric trauma — anatomical and physiological differences
- [ ] Obstetric emergencies in the ED — eclampsia, PPH, shoulder dystocia
- [ ] Psychiatric emergencies — acute behavioural disturbance, rapid tranquilisation

### Clinical Skills
- [ ] RSI — perform independently
- [ ] Surgical airway — cricothyroidotomy (supervised)
- [ ] Chest drain insertion — independent
- [ ] FAST exam — independent

---

!!! danger "Always use ABCDE"
    In any emergency, return to ABCDE when uncertain. Treat life threats as you find them — don't wait to complete the assessment before acting.
